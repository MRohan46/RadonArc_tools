How to Convert Octal to Hex

Step 1: Convert octal to binary (each digit to 3 bits)

755 = 111 101 101 = 111101101

Step 2: Group binary into 4-bit chunks (from right)

111101101 = 1 1110 1101 = 0001 1110 1101

Step 3: Convert each 4-bit group to hex

0001 = 1, 1110 = E, 1101 = D → 1ED

Octal to Hex Conversion

Two-Step Process

Since there's no direct 1:1 mapping between octal and hex, convert through binary: octal → binary (3 bits per digit) → hex (group binary into 4-bit chunks).

Why Binary First?

Binary is the common denominator. Octal groups binary in 3-bit chunks, hex in 4-bit chunks. Converting through binary ensures accuracy.

Common Uses

• Converting Unix permissions to hex
• Programming legacy systems
• Digital signal processing
• Understanding data representation

Common Questions

How do I convert octal to hex manually?

First convert octal to binary (each digit to 3 bits), then group the binary into 4-bit chunks (starting from the right), and convert each 4-bit group to hex. For example, 755 → 111101101 → 1 1110 1101 → 0001 1110 1101 → 1ED.

What is octal 777 in hex?

777 in octal = 111111111 in binary = 1 1111 1111 in 4-bit groups = 0001 1111 1111 = 1FF in hex. This represents full permissions in Unix (rwx for everyone).

Why can't I convert octal directly to hex?

Because octal groups binary in 3-bit chunks, while hex uses 4-bit chunks. There's no simple 1:1 mapping. Converting through binary ensures accuracy and is the standard method.

Do I need to pad binary when grouping?

Yes! When grouping binary into 4-bit chunks for hex conversion, you may need to add leading zeros to the leftmost group to make it 4 bits. For example, 111101101 becomes 0001 1110 1101.

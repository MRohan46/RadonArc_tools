How to Convert Hex to Octal

Step 1: Convert hex to binary (each digit to 4 bits)

1 = 0001, E = 1110, D = 1101 → 000111101101

Step 2: Group binary into 3-bit chunks (from right)

000111101101 = 000 111 101 101 = 0 7 5 5

Step 3: Remove leading zeros: 755

Hex to Octal Conversion

Two-Step Process

Convert through binary: hex → binary (4 bits per digit) → group binary into 3-bit chunks → octal. This ensures accuracy between different bases.

Binary Grouping

When grouping binary for octal, always start from the right. The leftmost group may have fewer than 3 bits, but that's fine (just drop leading zeros in final result).

Common Questions

How do I convert hex to octal manually?

First convert hex to binary (each digit to 4 bits), then group the binary into 3-bit chunks starting from the right, and convert each 3-bit group to octal. For example, 1ED → 0001 1110 1101 → 000 111 101 101 → 0 7 5 5 → 755.

What is FF in hex in octal?

FF in hex = 11111111 in binary = 11 111 111 in 3-bit groups = 011 111 111 = 377 in octal.

Why do we convert through binary?

Because hex uses 4-bit groups and octal uses 3-bit groups. Binary is the common denominator that makes conversion straightforward and accurate.

What happens to leading zeros in octal?

Leading zeros in the final octal number are typically dropped for readability. For example, 0755 is usually written as 755. Our converter shows the clean version without leading zeros.

How to Calculate Percentages

Finding X% of Y

Formula: (X ÷ 100) × Y
Example: 15% of 200 = (15 ÷ 100) × 200 = 30

X is What % of Y

Formula: (X ÷ Y) × 100
Example: 30 is what % of 200? = (30 ÷ 200) × 100 = 15%

Percentage Change

Formula: ((New - Old) ÷ Old) × 100
Example: From 100 to 150 = ((150-100) ÷ 100) × 100 = 50% increase

Common Questions

How do I calculate a 20% discount on $100?

Use "What is X% of Y": Enter 20% of 100 = $20. The final price is $100 - $20 = $80.

What's the difference between percentage increase and decrease?

Percentage increase calculates growth (new value is larger), while percentage decrease calculates reduction (new value is smaller).

How do I calculate my test score as a percentage?

Use "X is what % of Y": If you got 45 out of 60 correct, enter: 45 is what % of 60 = 75%

Can I calculate negative percentages?

Yes! A negative percentage indicates a decrease. For example, -25% means a 25% reduction.
